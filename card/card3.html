Decentralized identity in Web3 - how blockchain and NFT can achieve it

Key findings

Decentralized digital identity is a system in which users' personal data is stored in a single non-custodial identity wallet. So users can have full control over their digital identity, and not depend on centralized services to store and maintain information.

Using blockchain to maintain decentralized digital identity can provide proper security and privacy, effectively protecting the user's identity from data leakage and theft.

Today, the personal data that makes up a person's digital identity is often stored centrally and linked through devices, apps, and third-party services. In this model, users have no control over what personal data they choose to share and how, making them potentially more vulnerable to data breaches and cyberattacks.

With the advent of Web3, the way we interact on the Internet has changed. Blockchain-based applications operate independently and without intermediaries, creating a decentralized internet where the user can be the sole owner of their data. Web3 users can set up their profiles and store personal data in a single account that can be used for everything from accessing social networks to logging into crypto wallets.

What is decentralized identity

Decentralized Identity is a new ideology in which identities should only be held by the person they represent. Users can create and control their digital identities and not be dependent on third party service providers. Decentralized Digital Identity (DDID) is a decentralized system that aims to transform the current centralized identity management with blockchain technology.

For example, a digital credential standard called Verifiable Credentials (VC) allows you to store credentials in the form of tokens in a non-custodial wallet. Users may disclose all or some aspects of their identity and personal information to third parties such as the government, banks or schools at their discretion. At the same time, the presence of a single source facilitates data storage. Another example is Sovereign Identity (SSI), which consists of verified and authentic credentials linked to real verification data and managed in a decentralized way.

How decentralized identity works in Web3

Decentralized identity gives users full control over their personal data on the Internet. On Web3, people from all walks of life can express themselves and interact in new ways using tools like NFT and blockchain. It is they who can become decisive factors that open the door to a new world of freedom and great opportunities. In it, everyone can create a unique NFT avatar that will represent him and contain a unique identifier that is protected from forgery. All this makes interaction in digital communities safer.

Typically, a decentralized repository, such as a non-custodial identity wallet, is used to store a user's decentralized identifiers (DIDs). This could be a wallet app or browser extension that allows users to create their own decentralized identity and manage access to third party service providers. In this case, the users are the sole owners of the respective public and private cryptographic keys. Some wallets use other authentication methods to protect data. For example, sovereign identity (SSI) associates a user's credentials with real-world verification data such as biometrics and stores them on the blockchain. Thanks to this, users can control their digital identities themselves without the participation of third parties. More importantly, SSI stores information in non-custodial wallets that are solely controlled by users, which ensures the security of personal data.

To prove their identity in the Metaverse, users can sign a transaction with their private key or biometric data in applications that allow decentralized identification for authentication. The service provider will then use the decentralized identity shared by the user to look up the corresponding unique DID on the blockchain.

What are Soulbound Tokens (SBT)

Soulbound tokens (SBT) were proposed by one of the founders of Ethereum (ETH) Vitalik Buterin. They are non-transferable NFTs that are unique to a particular person and are forever tied to a digital wallet (Soul). SBTs are similar to achievement badges and can be used to store a variety of personal information such as resumes, diplomas, club memberships, and so on. They can be tracked publicly, so fake information is almost impossible. For example, a Web3 company hiring software developers might use SBT to validate candidate certificates. Or an artist can issue an NFT from their Soul Wallet to verify that the work is theirs.

With SBT, users can experience benefits that will change the way we think about social identity in real life. Such tokens can confirm the identity of a person and his reputation, which helps to establish trust in decentralized spaces.

The advent of SBT suggests that Web3 can be more than just a medium for financial assets. Such tokens provide information about users, thereby giving a sense of real human relationships in the digital world. After all, SBT describe the qualitative characteristics of a person: his achievements, abilities, membership in any organizations, and much more. They can change how people interact on the blockchain by combining resumes and social media profiles.

Binance recently announced that it is ready to release Binance Account Bound (BAB), the first ever SBT on the BNB Chain blockchain. This will be an additional feature that will allow Binance users who have passed identity verification to create BAB directly on their wallets.

Benefits of decentralized identity

Decentralized identity gives users full control over their digital data and allows them not to depend on a centralized server. With its help, they do not need to constantly enter a login and password, which makes it easier to manage identification and biometric data in the metaverse and the Web3 environment.

With decentralized identity, users can choose which part of the information to provide to third-party service providers. This puts control back in the hands of users, making data management easier with a single identity wallet.

Using a blockchain for decentralized identity also ensures proper security and privacy. Data can only be unlocked and viewed with valid digital signatures, protecting users from hacking and identity theft. At the same time, decentralized identification data stored on the blockchain is immutable and less vulnerable to attacks than data on centralized servers. Once recorded in a distributed network, information cannot be deleted or edited, so no external entities can modify user records.

Conclusion

Decentralized digital identity is one of the latest technologies that could play an important role in the Web3 revolution. With this identification, users can easily log into all their accounts without remembering a lot of logins and passwords. Its added benefit is increased security and data protection in the metaverse. At the same time, organizations can provide users with personalized services without violating their privacy. Perhaps decentralized digital identity will appear earlier than expected: startups and well-known companies are already engaged in this technology. For example, Microsoft's new Entra product allows users to verify, secure, and control all types of identities and access requests. However, the implementation of decentralized identity on a global scale will not happen overnight - it requires a joint effort of cooperation and coordination between organizations and governments.
